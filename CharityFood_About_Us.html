Who we are


Charity Food Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to fighting food insecurity among Latin American migrants in our community since May of 2023. Our mission is to provide nutritious meals and other forms of food assistance to migrant families and individuals who are struggling to make ends meet.

Our team is made up of dedicated staff members, volunteers, and community partners who are passionate about making a positive impact in the lives of Latin American migrants. We work closely with local food banks, farms, and other organizations to source fresh, healthy food and distribute it to those who need it most.


GOVERNING BOARD


Motoaki Tanigo


FOUNDING MEMBER AND PRESIDENT


Motoaki Tanigo, a successful CEO of an internet entertainment company, had always dreamed of living and working in the United States. He was born and raised in Tokyo, Japan, but had a passion for the culture and lifestyle of the US. After graduating from university in Japan, Tanigo decided to pursue his dream and move to the US in 1973. Over time, Tanigo's hard work and dedication paid off. He quickly rose through the ranks at his company and eventually became the CEO. Under his leadership, the company grew rapidly and became one of the most successful internet entertainment companies in the US.


Cory Barlog


BOARD MEMBER & TREASURER


Cory Barlog is a Financial Advisor at Capstone Past Financial and Insurance Service. Prior to joining he was the Wealth Director for BNY Mellon Wealth Management and spent 18 years at a large regional bank in town. He was born and raised in Las Vegas and is active in several organizations throughout the valley. He attended UNLV and lives in Summerlin with his wife, Monika.


Ella Freya


BOARD MEMBER & SECRETARY


Ella Freya lives in Hollywood, Florida, and runs an independent legal, civil rights and consulting practice serving public charter schools and their students across the country. She earned her J.D. from Yale Law School in 2020 and her B.A. from Stanford University in 2016..
